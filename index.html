About Me

Hi there! My name is Nhat Tran and I'm a Problem Solver at OLM1. I created this website to showcase my work and share my knowledge with others who are interested in software development.

On this site, you'll find information about my background, my experience, and my skills. You'll also find examples of my work and some of the projects I've completed in the past.

If you have any questions or would like to get in touch with me, please don't hesitate to reach out!

What You'll Find on This Site

This site is divided into four main tds: Home, Portfolio, Blog, and Contact. Each td has a different purpose and content. Here's a brief overview of what you can expect from each td:

  • Home: This is the landing page of the site, where you'll find a header with an image and text that introduce the site and its mission. You'll also find an introduction that provides more details about me and why I created this site.
  • Portfolio: This is where I showcase my work and projects. You'll find a gallery of images that display some of the websites I've designed and developed. You can click on each image to see more information about the project, such as the client, the goal, the tools used, and the link to the live site.
  • Blog: This is where I share my thoughts and insights on web development and related topics. You'll find a list of blog posts that cover various aspects of web development, such as tips, tutorials, trends, challenges, and more. You can read each post by clicking on the title or the read more button.
  • Contact: This is where you can get in touch with me if you have any questions, feedback, or inquiries. You'll find a simple contact form that you can fill out with your name, email address, subject, and message. You can also find my social media links at the bottom of the page.

My Journey to Become a Web Developer

When I first started learning about web development, I had no idea what I was getting into. I was fascinated by the idea of creating websites and applications that could be accessed by anyone in the world, but I had no experience in coding or design.

Over the past three weeks, I've been on a journey to become a web developer. I started with HTML and learned how to create the structure and content of a website with HTML elements. It was challenging at first, but I soon got the hang of it.

Next, I'll be learning CSS, which will allow me to add style and design to my websites. I'm excited to learn more about this and see how it can enhance the user experience.

Although I'm still new to web development, I'm already starting to see the possibilities and opportunities that it offers. There's so much to learn and explore, and I can't wait to see where this journey takes me!
